Study in Canada to unleash endless possibilities
Canada is currently ranked as the #1 country for quality of life. From vibrant cities to diverse campuses with welcoming communities, it is the perfect place for you to learn and grow.

Why CANADA?

1. Quality Education: Canada has a reputation for providing high-quality education, with many of its universities ranking among the best in the world. Students can choose from a wide range of programs and specializations in various fields, such as business, engineering, medicine, and more.

2. Safe and Welcoming Environment: Canada is known for its safe and welcoming environment, making it a great place for international students to study and live.

3. Diverse Culture: Canada is a culturally diverse country, and students from around the world are able to experience a mix of cultures and traditions. This can provide a unique learning experience and broaden a student's perspective.

4. Internationally Recognized Degrees: Canadian degrees are recognized and respected around the world, and students who graduate from Canadian universities have excellent career prospects.

5. Work Opportunities: International students in Canada are eligible to work part-time while they study and may be eligible for post-graduation work permits, allowing them to gain valuable work experience and build their professional network.

Study and Living Expenses:

1. Tuition Fees: Tuition fees in Canada can range from CAD 15,000 to CAD 40,000 per year, depending on the program of study and the university.

2. Living Expenses: Living expenses in Canada can range from CAD 12,000 to CAD 20,000 per year, depending on the student's lifestyle. This includes expenses such as accommodation, food, transportation, and entertainment.

3. Health Insurance: International students are required to have health insurance while studying in Canada, which can cost between CAD 500 and CAD 700 per year.

4. Miscellaneous Expenses: Miscellaneous expenses can include books, supplies, and other miscellaneous items, and can range from CAD 1,000 to CAD 2,000 per year.

Scholarships:

Scholarships for international students in Canada, as the amount and availability of scholarships, can vary greatly. The amount of a scholarship can depends on the specific scholarship, the level of study, and the academic field, among other factors. Additionally, the number of scholarships available may vary from year to year and from one institution to another.

However, it's worth noting that many scholarships for international students in Canada range from CAD 1,000 to CAD 20,000 or more. Some universities also offer tuition fee waivers for international students who receive scholarships.
